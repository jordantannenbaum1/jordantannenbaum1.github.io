Priced Out: Housing Cost
as the Engine of Homelessness

What it is
HUD's annual Point-in-Time count measures unsheltered homelessness across 400+ Continuums of Care. Joined to Zillow rent data and ACS income estimates by metro, this project asks whether housing cost predicts homelessness rates. The correlation is among the strongest in social policy data. Black Americans are 5x overrepresented in the homeless population relative to their share of the general population.

Policy Actions This Data Supports
  • Rank cities by homelessness-to-rent elasticity to prioritize where rental assistance funding produces the most reduction per dollar
  • Identify CoCs where racial overrepresentation is highest relative to local poverty rates, pointing to discrimination in shelter access rather than poverty alone
  • Model the unsheltered count reduction if fair market rents fell 10% in the 20 highest-cost metros
  • Make the quantitative case against criminalization post-Grants Pass by showing cities with bans have no better outcomes than those with housing-first approaches

-- int_rent_burden_coc.sql
select
    p.coc_number, p.coc_name, p.year,
    p.total_homeless, p.unsheltered_homeless,
    p.homeless_black_pct, p.total_population,
    round(p.total_homeless * 10000.0
        / nullif(p.total_population, 0), 2) as homeless_rate_per_10k,
    r.median_rent_monthly,
    i.median_hh_income,
    round((r.median_rent_monthly * 12)
        / nullif(i.median_hh_income, 0) * 12, 2) as rent_burden_months
from {{ ref('stg_pit_counts') }} p
left join {{ ref('int_coc_metro_xwalk') }} x using (coc_number)
left join {{ ref('stg_zillow_rent') }}  r on x.metro_cbsa = r.cbsa_code and p.year = r.year
left join {{ ref('stg_acs_income') }}  i on x.metro_cbsa = i.cbsa_code and p.year = i.year
