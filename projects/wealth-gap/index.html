What it is
The Federal Reserve tracks wealth distribution every quarter. Since 1989, the top 0.1% has gone from owning roughly the same share as the bottom 50% to owning more than ten times as much. This project models that trajectory longitudinally, then breaks the wealth gap open by race and gender using SCF microdata.

Policy Actions This Data Supports
  • Model the revenue impact of a 2% annual wealth tax on holdings above $50M to fund child poverty reduction
  • Identify the specific asset classes (unrealized equity, real estate, inheritances) driving the racial wealth gap for targeted reform
  • Quantify how much of the gap is explained by inheritance vs. lifetime earnings to inform estate tax policy
  • Benchmark the US wealth gap against peer nations to contextualize where legislative intervention has worked

-- int_wealth_shares.sql
with dfa as (
    select * from {{ ref('stg_dfa_wealth') }}
),
totals as (
    select
        quarter,
        sum(net_worth_billions) as total_wealth,
        sum(case when pctile_group = 'top_0_1'
            then net_worth_billions end) as top_01_wealth,
        sum(case when pctile_group = 'bottom_50'
            then net_worth_billions end) as bottom_50_wealth
    from dfa group by 1
)
select
    quarter,
    round(top_01_wealth    * 100.0 / total_wealth, 2) as top_01_pct,
    round(bottom_50_wealth * 100.0 / total_wealth, 2) as bottom_50_pct,
    round(top_01_wealth / nullif(bottom_50_wealth, 0), 1) as ratio
from totals order by 1
